Dark Sky Preservation

What is Dark Sky Preservation?

Dark Sky Preservation, also known as dark sky conservation or light pollution mitigation, is the effort to reduce light pollution and preserve the natural darkness of the night sky. It involves the use of responsible outdoor lighting practices and policies to protect the environment, human health, and astronomical observations.

Why is it Important?

Preserving dark skies has several important benefits:

  • Protecting Wildlife: Artificial light at night can disrupt the behavior and habitats of nocturnal animals, including birds, insects, and sea turtles. Dark skies help protect these species.
  • Energy Efficiency: Proper outdoor lighting reduces energy consumption and saves money. Shielded and well-designed lighting can illuminate areas effectively without wasting energy.
  • Human Health: Excessive artificial light at night can interfere with our circadian rhythms, disrupt sleep patterns, and contribute to health issues. Dark skies promote healthier sleep and well-being.
  • Astronomy and Science: Dark skies are essential for astronomical observations and scientific research. Light pollution hinders our understanding of the universe and limits our ability to study celestial objects.
  • Cultural and Aesthetic Value: Dark skies enhance the beauty of natural landscapes and provide opportunities for stargazing, which is a source of wonder and inspiration for people of all ages.

How Can You Help?

You can contribute to dark sky preservation by taking the following actions:

  1. Use Dark-Sky-Friendly Lighting: Install outdoor lighting fixtures that are designed to minimize light pollution. Use shielded lights that direct light downward and avoid over-illumination.
  2. Advocate for Dark Sky Policies: Support local ordinances and regulations that promote responsible lighting practices in your community.
  3. Reduce Unnecessary Lighting: Turn off lights when not in use and consider motion sensors or timers for security lighting.
  4. Spread Awareness: Educate others about the importance of dark sky preservation and its benefits.
  5. Support Dark Sky Parks: Visit and support designated Dark Sky Parks and reserves that prioritize night sky conservation.
