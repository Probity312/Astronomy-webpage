Black Holes

What is a Black Hole?

A black hole is a region in space where the gravitational pull is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape from it. This phenomenon occurs when a massive star collapses under its own gravity.

Characteristics of Black Holes

  • Massive gravitational pull
  • Invisible and dark, thus the name "black" hole
  • Event horizon - the boundary beyond which nothing can escape
  • Singularities - points of infinite density at the center

Formation of Black Holes

Black holes can form through various processes, including the gravitational collapse of massive stars, supernova explosions, or the accretion of matter in active galactic nuclei.

Interesting Facts

Did you know?

The term "black hole" was coined by physicist John Michell in 1783.

Exploring Black Holes

Scientists use telescopes, radio waves, and other instruments to study black holes indirectly since they are not directly observable. Advanced space missions, like the Hubble Space Telescope and the Event Horizon Telescope, have provided valuable insights into these mysterious cosmic objects.
