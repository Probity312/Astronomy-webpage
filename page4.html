Constellations

Constellations are patterns of stars that have been identified and named by various cultures throughout history. These star patterns are used to help navigate the night sky and tell stories and myths about the heavens.

There are 88 officially recognized constellations in the modern celestial sphere. Some of the most famous constellations include Orion, Ursa Major (the Big Dipper), and Cassiopeia.

Constellations can be observed from different parts of the world and may appear differently depending on your location and the time of year. They have been a source of inspiration for astronomers, poets, and storytellers for centuries.

Image source: visit.com

Whether you're a stargazer or simply curious about the wonders of the night sky, exploring constellations can be a fascinating and rewarding experience.
