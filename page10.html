Exoplanets

Exploring the mysteries of planets beyond our solar system.

What are Exoplanets?

Exoplanets, also known as extrasolar planets, are planets that exist outside our solar system. These planets orbit stars other than our Sun. They come in various sizes, compositions, and environments, and their discovery has revolutionized our understanding of the universe.

Discovery of Exoplanets

The first confirmed exoplanet was discovered in 1992, and since then, thousands of exoplanets have been identified. Scientists use various methods to detect exoplanets, including the transit method, radial velocity method, and direct imaging.

Significance

Studying exoplanets is crucial for several reasons:

  • Understanding the diversity of planetary systems in the universe.
  • Investigating the potential for habitable environments and the search for extraterrestrial life.
  • Gaining insights into the formation and evolution of planets.

Current Missions

NASA and other space agencies have launched missions dedicated to studying exoplanets, such as the Kepler Space Telescope and the TESS mission (Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite).
